About #NotYourAsianSidekick

On December 15, 2013, Suey Park, a freelance writer and organizer, launched the hashtag #NotYourAsianSidekick on Twitter, which started as a conversation between a group of friends who had often found it difficult to find a place and a voice in existing conversations about justice in the Asian American community. Before long, thousands of people were Tweeting using the hashtag, making it trend globally for around 24 hours. Participants Tweeted about everything from media representation of Asian women to the way the prison industrial complex erases Asian Americans and Pacific Islanders in its demographic tracking.

Since mid-December, Suey has teamed up with 18MillionRising.org and a bevy of other allies to work on projects to continue the conversation, deepen the analysis, and elevate the vision of what #NotYourAsianSidekick is and can be. #NotYourAsianSidekick exists to be an inclusive space for sharing concepts and practices to fuel real organizing for real change.

Together, we can grow a hashtag into a movement.

About the Forums

The Google Hangouts forums are a move in that direction, bringing conversations between artists, activists, and academics straight to your computer screen. We want to talk about everything from using Twitter as a platform for agitation to interracial solidarity to disability to queerness. We'd love to hear your ideas for future forums, and if you have an idea for a new online project, feel free to get in touch.
